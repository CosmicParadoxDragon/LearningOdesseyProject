Science

  1. Basic Arithmetic: Arithmetic is the foundation of mathematics, involving operations like addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division.
  2. Algebra: Algebra deals with symbols and the rules for manipulating those symbols to solve equations and study patterns.
  3. Geometry: Geometry focuses on the properties and relationships of shapes, angles, and space.
  4. Calculus: Calculus studies change and motion, involving concepts like derivatives and integrals.
  5. Statistics: Statistics deals with data collection, analysis, and interpretation, including concepts like mean, median, and standard deviation.
  6. Linear Algebra: Linear algebra explores vector spaces and linear equations, essential in fields like physics and computer graphics.
  7. Differential Equations: Differential equations describe change and are used in physics, engineering, and biology.
  8. Quantum Mechanics: Quantum mechanics is a branch of physics that studies the behavior of particles on a very small scale.
  9. General Relativity: General relativity is Einstein's theory of gravity, describing how massive objects warp spacetime.
  10. Number Theory: Number theory explores properties and relationships of integers, including prime numbers.
